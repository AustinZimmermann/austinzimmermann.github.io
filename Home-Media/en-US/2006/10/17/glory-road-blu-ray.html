Glory Road

Based on a true story, Glory Road follows Don Haskins (Josh Lucas), the coach of the Texas Western Miners. In 1966, Haskins made history by starting the first all-Black lineup in NCAA history, leading them to a championship game against the heavily favored, all-white Kentucky Wildcats. The film explores the racial tensions of the era and the team's journey toward a landmark victory in American sports.

Historical Format Context

Released in the first few months of the Blu-ray vs. HD-DVD format war, Glory Road was a key title for Disney. It was often used to showcase the superiority of the AVC MPEG-4 codec and the power of uncompressed PCM audio, which provided a significant leap over the compressed audio found on standard DVDs.
