Movie Synopsis

Michael Newman (Adam Sandler) is a workaholic architect struggling to balance his career and family life. When he meets a mysterious inventor named Morty (Christopher Walken) at a Bed Bath & Beyond, he is gifted a “universal remote” that allows him to literally fast-forward, rewind, and pause his life. However, the remote soon starts “learning” Michael’s habits and begins skipping through his life automatically, forcing him to realize the moments he’s been missing.

Historical Context

Released just four months after the film’s theatrical debut, this Blu-ray was a “flagship” title for Sony's new high-definition format.

It was praised at the time for its use of the BD-50 disc format, which helped address early criticisms of the Blu-ray format regarding storage space and image quality compared to the rival HD-DVD format. The uncompressed PCM audio track and high-bitrate video made it a go-to demonstration disc for early adopters in late 2006.
