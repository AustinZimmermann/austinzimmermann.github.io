Click is a 2006 American fantasy comedy film directed by Frank Coraci, written by Steve Koren and Mark O’Keefe, and produced by Adam Sandler, who also stars in the lead role. The film co-stars Kate Beckinsale as his wife Donna and Christopher Walken as Morty, an eccentric inventor.

The story follows Michael Newman, an overworked architect who acquires a “universal remote” that allows him to fast-forward, pause, and rewind his own life. While initially helpful, the remote eventually begins to override his choices, leading Michael to realize the importance of family and the moments he previously ignored.

Contents

Plot [edit]

Michael Newman is a hardworking architect whose life is consumed by his desire to be promoted by his demanding boss, Mr. Ammer. This workaholic nature often causes him to neglect his wife, Donna, and their two children, Ben and Samantha. One night, frustrated by the number of remotes in his house, Michael goes to Bed Bath & Beyond to find a universal remote.

He meets a mysterious clerk named Morty, who gives him a “universal remote” for free but warns him that it cannot be returned. Michael soon discovers the remote has supernatural powers: he can mute the family dog, change his skin color, and fast-forward through arguments or illnesses. However, Morty reveals that the remote “learns” Michael’s preferences. It begins to automatically skip through parts of his life Michael previously chose to fast-forward, including years of his career and family growth.

Production [edit]

The film was a collaboration between Sandler’s Happy Madison Productions and Columbia Pictures. Filming took place primarily in Los Angeles, California. The special effects, particularly the aging makeup for Sandler and the futuristic settings, were a significant part of the production budget.

Reception [edit]

Click received mixed reviews from critics. On Rotten Tomatoes, the film has an approval rating of 33%. While critics praised the emotional weight of the third act, many found the humor in the first half to be juvenile. Despite critical reception, the film was a commercial success, grossing over $240 million worldwide.

The film was notably nominated for the Academy Award for Best Makeup, marking the first time a Happy Madison production received an Oscar nomination.
