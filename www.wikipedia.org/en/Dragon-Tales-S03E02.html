“Rise and Bloom / Super Snow Day” is the second episode of the third season of the American-Canadian animated children’s television series Dragon Tales, and the 66th episode overall. The episode consists of two segments, both featuring the new character Enrique as he continues to explore Dragon Land.

Rise and Bloom

In the first segment, “Rise and Bloom”, Emmy and Max take Enrique to witness a rare Dragon Land event: the blooming of the “Sunrise Flowers”. However, the group discovers that Enrique has a difficult time waking up early.

The dragons (Ord, Cassie, Zak, and Wheezie) attempt various magical and non-magical ways to help Enrique stay alert so he doesn't miss the spectacular event. The story emphasizes the importance of patience and preparation when trying to participate in special group activities.

Super Snow Day

In the second segment, “Super Snow Day”, Dragon Land is covered in a deep, magical snow. While Max and Emmy are excited, Enrique is hesitant because he has never experienced a cold “snow day” like this in his home country.

The dragons show Enrique the fun side of winter, including sledding and building snow-dragons. Enrique eventually learns to appreciate the change in weather, and the episode introduces Spanish vocabulary related to winter and feelings.

Educational themes

Consistent with the third season’s goals, the episode focuses on emotional intelligence and cross-cultural adjustment. “Super Snow Day” specifically addresses the anxiety of experiencing new environmental phenomena, while “Rise and Bloom” focuses on social responsibility within a group.
