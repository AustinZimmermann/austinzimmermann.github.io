Halo: Combat Evolved is a 2001 first-person shooter game developed by Bungie and published by Microsoft Game Studios. It was released as a launch title for the Xbox video game console on November 15, 2001. The game is the first installment in the Halo franchise. Set in the 26th century, players assume the role of the Master Chief, a cybernetically enhanced supersoldier, accompanied by the artificial intelligence Cortana.

Gameplay

Halo: Combat Evolved is a first-person shooter in which players experience gameplay in a 3D environment. The player can move around and look up, down, left, or right. The game features vehicles, ranging from armored tanks and alien hovercraft to human transport vehicles. The game switches to a third-person perspective during vehicle use for pilots and mounted gun operators.

Synopsis

Setting

The game takes place in a science fiction universe created by Bungie. According to the story, the realization of faster-than-light travel allowed the human race to colonize other planets after the overpopulation of Earth. A cornerstone of these efforts is the planet Reach, an interstellar hub and a center of military activity. A collective of alien races, the Covenant, began a holy war against humanity, declaring them an affront to their gods.

Plot

The game begins as the UNSC ship Pillar of Autumn exits slipspace near a mysterious ring-shaped space station, known as “Halo”. A Covenant fleet attacks the ship, forcing Captain Keyes to initiate the “Cole Protocol”, abandoned ship, and awaken the Master Chief from cryosleep. Master Chief and Cortana escape to the surface of the ring, where they must rally surviving Marines and uncover the true purpose of the Halo installation while fighting back the Covenant and a parasitic threat known as the Flood.

Development

The development of Halo: Combat Evolved began in 1997. Initially, it was a real-time strategy game for macOS and Windows, but it morphed into a third-person shooter before becoming a first-person shooter after Microsoft acquired Bungie in 2000. Steve Jobs famously announced the game at Macworld Expo in 1999 before it became an Xbox exclusive.

Reception

Halo was a critical and commercial success, often cited as one of the greatest and most influential video games of all time. It was the Xbox’s “killer app”, selling over five million copies by 2005. Critics praised its gameplay, story, and the inclusion of a robust multiplayer mode via System Link.
