Fire Stingray

The Fire Stingray (Japanese: ファイアスティングレイ, Hepburn: Faia Sutingurei) is a heavy-weight machine piloted by Samurai Goroh. It debuted in the original F-Zero and has appeared in almost every iteration of the series since. Known for having one of the highest top speeds in the series and excellent cornering stability, it requires masterful driving due to its abysmally poor acceleration.

Characteristics ▼

The Fire Stingray sports a distinct, wide, aerodynamic body designed by T.G.S. Due to its twin ion engines, it holds a tremendous amount of mass, making it highly durable and giving it remarkable grip on the track. In titles like F-Zero GX, it boasts a Body rating of A, a Grip rating of B, but a Boost rating of D.

Because of its weight and engine setup, if the Fire Stingray loses speed due to a collision or hitting dirt, it takes a significant amount of time to recover its top speed. Advanced players often use boosting techniques or blast-turning to mitigate this weakness.

History ▼

F-Zero (SNES): The machine is one of the four original selectable crafts. It boasts the highest top speed of the standard four, making it highly preferred for time attacks on straightaway-heavy courses like Mute City and Big Blue.

F-Zero X: Samurai Goroh returns with the Fire Stingray. It remains a heavyweight contender capable of knocking lighter machines off the track.

F-Zero GX: The Fire Stingray makes a fully realized 3D return. Its stats are defined as Body: A, Boost: D, Grip: B. It is favored by players who excel at maintaining top speed through technical corners.

Trivia ▼

  • The Fire Stingray has the number 05 painted on its sides.
  • In F-Zero X, a lighter, alternate version of the machine called the Stingray is piloted by Goroh’s son, Daigoroh.
  • Its engine is notoriously loud, reflecting its aggressive pilot’s personality.
