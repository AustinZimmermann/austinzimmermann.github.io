The Super Piranha (Japanese: スーパーピラニア, Hepburn: Sūpā Pirania) is a high-performance F-Zero machine piloted by the intergalactic pop sensation Kate Alen. First appearing in F-Zero X, it is known for its distinctive teal-colored body and magenta-tinted cockpit, reflecting the glamorous and energetic persona of its owner.

Commissioned by Kate Alen herself using the massive fortune she accumulated during her musical career, the machine was designed by the engineering group Team Power Voice. It was built with a specific focus on balanced performance and a high-fidelity soundproof cockpit, allowing Kate to listen to her own music at maximum volume while racing at supersonic speeds.

Characteristics

The Super Piranha’s design is sleek and fish-like, fitting its namesake. It features a wide, flat chassis with dual aerodynamic fins at the rear that aid in stability during high-speed turns. One of its most unique features is the “Hi-Fi” cockpit system, which utilizes active noise-cancellation technology to drown out the roar of the twin plasma engines, providing Kate Alen with a studio-quality listening environment.

Game Performance

“A machine emphasizing both handling and safety. Kate’s tastes are reflected in everything from the design to the performance.” —Official Profile

The Super Piranha is widely considered a “stable” machine, ideal for intermediate players. Its stats are balanced across the board, providing reliable performance without any extreme weaknesses, though it lacks the explosive boost power of top-tier machines like the Death Anchor.

Strengths

  • Consistent grip in tight corners.
  • Durable body (B Rating) can handle aggressive bumping.
  • Reliable acceleration profile.

Weaknesses

  • Underwhelming boost duration (C Rating).
  • Lower top speed compared to heavier machines.
  • Lacks the agility for extreme slide-turning maneuvers.

F-Zero X

In its debut, the Super Piranha shared its Body, Boost, and Grip ratings (B/C/B) with the legendary Blue Falcon, making it a viable alternative for players who preferred Kate Alen’s aesthetic over Captain Falcon’s. It is unlocked by earning nine ‘X’ marks in the Grand Prix.

F-Zero GX

In F-Zero GX, the Super Piranha weighs 1010 kg. It maintains its reputation as a balanced machine. In Master Mode, it is often seen in the middle of the pack, rarely falling behind but struggling to break into the top 3 against specialized machines.

Trivia

  • One of the Super Piranha’s alternate color schemes in F-Zero X is a direct reference to Samus Aran’s gunship from the Metroid series.
  • In the F-Zero GP Legend anime, the Super Piranha is often shown with glowing magenta neon lights during night races.
  • The name “Team Power Voice” was mistranslated as “Team Power Boys” in several early North American strategy guides.
  • Kate Alen reportedly spent over 500 million Space Credits on the audio equipment alone, which weighs as much as some smaller machine engines.
