The Mad Wolf (Japanese: マッドウルフ, Hepburn: Maddo Urufu) is a high-performance racing machine in the F-Zero series. It is the signature vehicle of Billy, a monkey who was trained specifically for the F-Zero Grand Prix. The machine made its debut in F-Zero X and has appeared in every major installment since.

Designed by the legendary veteran pilot Silver Neelsen, the Mad Wolf is known for its balanced performance, prioritizing boost capability and durable body construction over raw grip.

Performance

The Mad Wolf is often categorized as a “Medium” machine. Its primary strength lies in its Boost duration and Body strength, making it a formidable opponent in the middle of a pack where collisions are frequent. However, its Grip is notably lower than other top-tier machines, requiring skilled slide-turning techniques to navigate sharp corners without losing velocity.

Strengths

  • High top speed when boosting.
  • Durable hull can withstand multiple hits.
  • Excellent straight-line acceleration.

Weaknesses

  • Subpar cornering grip.
  • Tendency to drift outward on hairpins.
  • Weight can make it feel sluggish in tight sectors.

History

F-Zero X

In its first appearance, the Mad Wolf was introduced as machine number 11. It was balanced for players who wanted a machine that didn’t explode after one mistake. Its stats were listed as B/B/C, establishing the archetype it would follow in future games.

F-Zero GX

The GX version of the Mad Wolf received a significant visual overhaul, featuring more intricate aerodynamic fins and a more aggressive "snout" design. In this game, Billy’s backstory was expanded, revealing that the machine's control system was uniquely calibrated for his simian reflexes.

Trivia

  • The Mad Wolf is one of the few machines in the series designed by a rival pilot (Silver Neelsen) rather than a specialized engineering firm like Talsun.
  • In the F-Zero: GP Legend anime, the Mad Wolf is often seen racing alongside the Iron Tiger.
  • The name “Mad Wolf” is a reference to Billy’s aggressive but calculated racing style, despite him being a monkey.
  • In F-Zero GX, the engine noise of the Mad Wolf has a distinct high-pitched whine compared to the roar of the Blue Falcon.
