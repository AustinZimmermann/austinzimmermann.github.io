Wild Goose

The Wild Goose (Japanese: ワイルドグース, Hepburn: Wairudo Gūsu) is a machine built by the Poripoto army and piloted by the ex-soldier Pico. It is machine number 06 in the F-Zero Grand Prix. Known for its incredible durability and aggressive driving style, the Wild Goose is a staple vehicle in the F-Zero series.

Characteristics

The Wild Goose is heavily armored, making it one of the most durable machines on the circuit (boasting an ‘A’ rating in Body). This makes it exceptional for aggressive tactics, such as knocking rival racers off the track or surviving collisions. However, its heavy weight (1,620 kg) results in a poor grip rating (‘C’), meaning it is prone to sliding on tight corners. Its boost is slightly above average ('B').

History

F-Zero (SNES)

In the original F-Zero, the Wild Goose is one of the four playable machines available from the start. It is characterized by its high top speed and difficulty in cornering, recommended only for advanced players.

F-Zero X

The machine returns in F-Zero X with its signature green color scheme and aggressive stats intact. It is highly effective in Death Races due to its heavy body, easily dispatching lighter machines.

F-Zero GX

In F-Zero GX, the Wild Goose retains its classic ‘A’ Body, ‘B’ Boost, and ‘C’ Grip ratings. It is frequently seen rivaling Captain Falcon's Blue Falcon and Samurai Goroh’s Fire Stingray in the game’s story mode.

Trivia

  • The Wild Goose’s chassis resembles a military hover-tank, referencing Pico’s background in the Poripoto army.
  • In the anime F-Zero: GP Legend, the Wild Goose is often depicted attacking other racers with hidden weaponry.
