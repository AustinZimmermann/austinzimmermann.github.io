# Reading, Pennsylvania

<img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/ReadingPennsylvaniaSkyline.jpg/960px-ReadingPennsylvaniaSkyline.jpg" height="250px">

**Reading** (pronounced *RED-ing*) is a city in and the county seat of **Berks County**, Pennsylvania, United States. With a population of 95,112 as of the 2020 census, it is the fourth-largest city in Pennsylvania. Located in the southeastern part of the state, it serves as the principal city of the Greater Reading Area.

---

## History

### Founding and Early Growth

Reading was platted in **1748** by Richard and Thomas Penn, the sons of William Penn. It was named after the town of Reading in Berkshire, England. During the **French and Indian War**, Reading served as a military base for a chain of forts along the Blue Mountain.

### The Industrial Revolution

In the 19th century, Reading became a major industrial hub, primarily due to:

* **The Reading Railroad:** Formally the Philadelphia and Reading Railroad, it became one of the largest corporations in the world.
* **Iron and Steel:** The presence of anthracite coal and iron ore deposits led to a massive manufacturing boom.
* **Textiles:** For much of the 20th century, Reading was a world leader in hosiery and textile production.

## Geography and Climate

Reading is situated approximately midway between Philadelphia and Harrisburg. It is nestled in the **Schuylkill River Valley**, bordered to the east by **Mount Penn** and to the west by **Neversink Mountain**.

## Landmark Attractions

The city is home to several unique cultural and historical sites:

* **The Pagoda:** Located atop Mount Penn, this seven-story Japanese-style pagoda was completed in 1908 and serves as the symbol of the city.
* **Reading Public Museum:** Features a diverse collection including fine art, armor, and a 2,000-year-old mummy.
* **FirstEnergy Stadium:** Home to the **Reading Fightin Phils**, the Double-A affiliate of the Philadelphia Phillies.
* **GoggleWorks Center for the Arts:** One of the largest interactive community arts centers in the country, housed in a former safety goggle factory.

---

## Transportation

Reading has historically been a transportation nexus. Today, it is served by:

* **Roads:** Major routes including **US 222**, **US 422**, and **PA 12** (The Warren Street Bypass).
* **Air:** **Reading Regional Airport** (Carl A. Spaatz Field) serves general aviation and regional cargo.
* **Bus:** **BARTA** (Berks Area Regional Transportation Authority) provides local transit throughout the city and county.
