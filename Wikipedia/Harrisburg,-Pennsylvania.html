Harrisburg (/ˈhærɪsbɜːrɡ/ HARR-iss-burg) is the capital city of the U.S. commonwealth of Pennsylvania. It is the county seat of Dauphin County and lies on the east bank of the Susquehanna River, approximately 105 miles (169 km) west-northwest of Philadelphia.

With a population of 50,099 at the 2020 census, it is the ninth-most populous city in Pennsylvania. The Harrisburg–Carlisle metropolitan statistical area is the fourth-most populous metropolitan area in the state. Harrisburg played a notable role in American history during the Westward Migration, the American Civil War, and the Industrial Revolution.

History

Early settlement

The site along the Susquehanna River where Harrisburg is located is thought to have been inhabited by Native Americans as early as 3000 BC. In 1719, John Harris, Sr., an English trader, settled here and established a ferry across the river. The settlement was long known as “Harris’s Ferry”.

In 1785, John Harris, Jr. laid out a town on his father’s land. The town was briefly renamed Louisburg in honor of Louis XVI of France, but John Harris refused to sell the land for the county seat unless the name was changed back to Harrisburg.

Selection as capital

Harrisburg was incorporated as a borough in 1791 and was named the Pennsylvania state capital in October 1812. The strategic location near the river and the gaps in the Appalachian Mountains made it a hub for transportation and commerce.

Geography

Harrisburg is located at 40°16′11″N 76°52′32″W. The city is situated on the east bank of the Susquehanna River. Directly to the north is the Blue Mountain ridge of the Appalachian Mountains. According to the United States Census Bureau, the city has a total area of 11.4 square miles (29.6 km²), of which 8.1 square miles (21.0 km²) is land.

Demographics

As of the 2020 Census, there were 50,099 people residing in the city. The racial makeup of the city was 44.8% African American, 26.5% White, 3.6% Asian, and 0.5% Native American. 25.8% of the population were Hispanic or Latino of any race.

Economy

The economy of Harrisburg is heavily influenced by the presence of the state government. Other major employers include healthcare services, manufacturing, and the food industry. Nearby Hershey, Pennsylvania, is home to The Hershey Company, which provides significant regional economic stability.
