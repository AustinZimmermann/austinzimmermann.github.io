# Princess Daisy

**Princess Daisy** (Japanese: デイジー姫, Hepburn: *Deijī-hime*) is a fictional character in the *Mario* franchise, created by Nintendo. She debuted in the 1989 Game Boy game *Super Mario Land* as the ruler of Sarasaland, where she is kidnapped by the alien Tatanga and rescued by Mario. Known for her tomboyish personality, energetic demeanor, and signature yellow-and-orange color scheme, Daisy has become a staple character in the franchise’s spin-off titles.

## Concept and Creation

Daisy was created by Gunpei Yokoi, the producer of *Super Mario Land* and the inventor of the Game Boy. Because *Super Mario Land* was developed by Nintendo R&D1 rather than Shigeru Miyamoto’s Nintendo EAD, the developers decided to create a new setting (Sarasaland) and a new damsel in distress, rather than using the traditional Mushroom Kingdom and Princess Peach (then known as Princess Toadstool).

Daisy’s early design featured a red crown, a yellow dress with white accents, and long brown hair. As she transitioned into 3D games, her design was modernized. In *Mario Party 4* (2002), she received a distinct redesign that shortened her hair, introduced her signature floral motifs, and established her modern orange-and-yellow color palette.

> **Note:** Unlike Princess Peach’s gentle and traditionally regal demeanor, Nintendo characterizes Daisy as a “tomboy” who is loud, highly competitive, and energetic, often celebrating with boisterous cheers.

## Appearances

### Main Series Games

* **Super Mario Land (1989):** Daisy’s debut role. She is kidnapped by the alien Tatanga, prompting Mario to travel through the four kingdoms of Sarasaland to rescue her.
* **Super Mario Run (2016):** Added in a post-launch update as a playable character. She possesses a unique double-jump ability.
* **Super Mario Bros. Wonder (2023):** Marks her first appearance as a fully playable character in a mainline, traditional *Super Mario* platformer.

### Spin-off Games

After a long hiatus following her debut, Daisy returned to the franchise in *Mario Tennis* (2000) for the Nintendo 64 to serve as a doubles partner for Peach. Since then, she has been a mainstay in almost all *Mario* spin-off titles, including:

* **Mario Kart series:** A regular playable racer since *Mario Kart: Double Dash!!* (2003), often placed in the medium or heavy weight classes.
* **Mario Party series:** A consistent playable character since *Mario Party 3* (2000).
* **Mario Sports titles:** Appears in all *Mario Golf*, *Mario Tennis*, *Mario Strikers*, and *Mario Baseball* games, where her tomboyish nature translates into high-power or high-technique stats.

### Super Smash Bros. Series

Daisy appears as an “Echo Fighter” of Princess Peach in *Super Smash Bros. Ultimate* (2018). While she shares Peach’s moveset, she has her own distinct animations, voice lines, and visual effects (such as flower pedals instead of hearts). Prior to *Ultimate*, she appeared as an alternate color palette for Peach and as collectible trophies.

## Reception

Princess Daisy generally receives positive reception from fans and critics. While some early critiques labeled her as a mere palette swap of Princess Peach, she has developed a dedicated fanbase that appreciates her outgoing, boisterous, and competitive personality. Her inclusion as a playable character in *Super Mario Bros. Wonder* was highly celebrated by the gaming community, as it validated her status as a core member of the main *Mario* cast after decades of being relegated mostly to spin-offs.
