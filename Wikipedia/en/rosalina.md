# Rosalina (*Mario*)

**Rosalina**, known as **Rosetta** (ロゼッタ, *Rozetta*) in Japan, is a fictional character in the *Mario* franchise by Nintendo. She debuted in the 2007 Wii game *Super Mario Galaxy*, where she serves as a non-player character who resides in the Comet Observatory, the game’s hub world. She is the adoptive mother of the Lumas, a fictional species of star-like creatures, and the watcher of the cosmos.

Since her debut, Rosalina has become a recurring character in the *Mario* series, frequently appearing as a playable character in spin-off games such as the *Mario Kart*, *Mario Party*, and *Mario Tennis* series, as well as the crossover fighting game franchise *Super Smash Bros.* She also appeared as a core playable character in the mainline platformer *Super Mario 3D World*.

## Concept and Creation

Rosalina was created by Yoshiaki Koizumi, the director of *Super Mario Galaxy*. During the game’s development, the team wanted to create a new female character to act as a guide for Mario in space. Early concept art depicted Rosalina with a more traditional princess design, sharing strong similarities to Princess Peach. However, to distinguish her, her design was altered to feature a more mature and melancholic appearance, reflecting her solitary life in space.

She is depicted as a tall, fair-skinned woman with platinum blonde hair, part of which obscures her right eye. She wears a cyan, off-the-shoulder evening gown, a silver crown adorned with a star, and holds a star-topped wand. Unlike Peach and Daisy, who represent earthbound kingdoms, Rosalina’s design incorporates celestial motifs.

## Appearances

### *Super Mario* series

Rosalina makes her first appearance in *Super Mario Galaxy*, where her home, the Comet Observatory, is attacked by Bowser. She enlists Mario’s help to recover the stolen Grand Stars and rescue Princess Peach. Throughout the game, she acts as a guide and ally. She returns in a more minor capacity in the sequel, *Super Mario Galaxy 2* (2010), appearing as a “Cosmic Spirit” to help players who struggle with levels, and in person at the end of the game.

In 2013’s *Super Mario 3D World* for the Wii U, Rosalina makes her debut as a playable character in a mainline *Mario* platformer. She is unlocked as the fifth playable character after completing the main story and features a unique “Spin Attack” ability derived from the *Galaxy* games.

### Spin-offs and Crossovers

Rosalina has been a staple in *Mario* spin-off titles since *Mario Kart Wii* (2008), where she appeared as a heavy-weight unlockable driver, accompanied by a Luma. She has subsequently appeared in every console *Mario Kart* game.

She is also a regular playable character in the *Mario Party* series (beginning with *Mario Party 10*), the *Mario Golf* and *Mario Tennis* series, and various *Mario & Sonic at the Olympic Games* titles.

Outside of the *Mario* franchise, Rosalina and Luma appear as a tandem fighting duo in the *Super Smash Bros.* series, making their debut in *Super Smash Bros. for Nintendo 3DS / Wii U* (2014) and returning in *Super Smash Bros. Ultimate* (2018). Their gameplay revolves around controlling both Rosalina and the Luma simultaneously to trap opponents and control the stage.

## Backstory

Unlike most characters in the *Mario* franchise, Rosalina has a detailed origin story, which is told in *Super Mario Galaxy* through an unlockable picture book titled “Rosalina’s Storybook”. The book reveals that as a young girl, Rosalina found a rusted spaceship containing a Luma who was looking for its mother. To help the Luma, Rosalina repaired the ship and traveled into space.

Over the years, she encountered more Lumas and eventually realized they were celestial bodies waiting to be reborn as stars and planets. Embracing her new role, she built a home for them, which became the Comet Observatory, and resolved to become their adoptive mother. The storybook is notable for its surprisingly poignant and emotional tone, revealing that Rosalina’s own mother had passed away before she left her home planet.

## Reception

Rosalina has received highly positive reception from critics and fans. She is frequently praised for her unique design, calm demeanor, and the emotional depth of her backstory, which is considered a rarity in the *Mario* franchise. *Destructoid* highlighted her as a standout character, noting that her melancholy backstory gave her a level of characterization unseen in her peers. She is consistently ranked as one of the most popular female characters in Nintendo’s roster, alongside Princess Peach and Samus Aran.
