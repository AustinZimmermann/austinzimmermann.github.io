# Princess Peach

<img src="https://www.mariowiki.com/images/7/7c/Peach_Posing_Alt_3D_Artwork.png" height="250px">

**Princess Peach** (Japanese: ピーチ姫, Hepburn: *Pīchi-hime*), originally known as **Princess Toadstool** outside of Japan, is a fictional character in Nintendo’s *Mario* franchise, created by Shigeru Miyamoto. Introduced in the 1985 original *Super Mario Bros.* installment, she is the princess and ruler of the Mushroom Kingdom, where she resides in her castle along with her loyal Toad subjects.

Traditionally, Peach’s primary role was the damsel in distress, frequently kidnapped by the franchise’s primary antagonist, Bowser. However, her character has evolved significantly over the decades. She has frequently appeared as a playable character in mainline games, spin-offs, and her own standalone titles, demonstrating her own agency, magical abilities, and leadership skills.

***

## Concept and Creation
Princess Peach was created by Shigeru Miyamoto for *Super Mario Bros.* (1985). Miyamoto’s initial design was later refined by Nintendo illustrator Yoichi Kotabe, who finalized her distinct appearance: long blonde hair, large blue eyes, a tall frame, and a pink evening gown.

In Japan, her name has always been Princess Peach. However, in early North American and European localizations, she was renamed “Princess Toadstool”. Nintendo officially transitioned her western name to Princess Peach with the release of *Super Mario 64* (1996), though the name “Toadstool” was occasionally referenced in subsequent years.

***

## Characteristics
Peach is characterized by her gentle, polite, and optimistic demeanor. Despite her frequent kidnappings, she rarely shows fear or panic, often finding ways to assist Mario from afar (such as sending him items or letters).

When playable, she often possesses unique abilities that distinguish her from Mario and Luigi:

* **Floating:** First introduced in *Super Mario Bros. 2*, Peach has the signature ability to hover mid-air for a short period, making her highly maneuverable in platforming.
* **Heart Magic:** She frequently uses heart-based magic to heal herself and allies, or to clear away enemies, especially in role-playing titles.
* **Perry the Parasol:** In *Super Princess Peach* and the *Super Smash Bros.* series, she wields a magical parasol for attacking, floating, and shielding.
* **Emotion Magic:** In *Super Princess Peach*, she utilizes her emotions (Joy, Gloom, Rage, and Calm) as magical powers to solve puzzles and defeat enemies.

***

## Appearances
### Main *Super Mario* Series
Peach made her debut in *Super Mario Bros.* (1985) as the kidnapped ruler of the Mushroom Kingdom. She became a playable character for the first time in *Super Mario Bros. 2* (1988). While she returned to her damsel role in many subsequent 2D and 3D titles (such as *Super Mario World*, *Super Mario 64*, and *Super Mario Galaxy*), she returned as a main playable character in *Super Mario 3D World* (2013) and *Super Mario Bros. Wonder* (2023).

### Standalone Games
Peach has been the protagonist of two major standalone games:

1. ***Super Princess Peach* (2006):** A Nintendo DS platformer where the roles are reversed, and Peach must rescue Mario, Luigi, and Toad from Bowser using the power of her emotions.
2. ***Princess Peach: Showtime!* (2024):** A Nintendo Switch action-adventure game where Peach teams up with a guardian named Stella to save the Sparkle Theater. She uses various transformations (such as Swordfighter Peach, Detective Peach, and Ninja Peach) to overcome obstacles.

### Spin-offs and Other Games
Peach is a staple in almost every *Mario* spin-off franchise. She is a core roster member in the *Mario Kart*, *Mario Party*, *Mario Tennis*, and *Mario Golf* series.

She has also been a featured fighter in the *Super Smash Bros.* series since *Super Smash Bros. Melee* (2001), representing the *Mario* franchise alongside characters like Mario, Luigi, and Bowser.

***

## In Other Media
Beyond video games, Princess Peach has appeared in various forms of media:

* **Comics and Manga:** She appears extensively in the *Super Mario Adventures* comic and *Super Mario-Kun* manga.
* **Animation:** She was a primary character in the *The Super Mario Bros. Super Show!* (1989) and its subsequent animated series.
* **Film:** In the 2023 animated film, *The Super Mario Bros. Movie*, Peach was voiced by Anya Taylor-Joy. This iteration of the character was heavily praised for being a proactive, highly capable leader and mentor to Mario, rather than a damsel in distress.

***

## Reception and Legacy
Princess Peach is one of the most recognizable female characters in video game history. While she has been the subject of critical discussion regarding the “damsel in distress” trope in media, critics and fans have also praised Nintendo’s efforts to evolve her character over time. Her inclusion as a powerful playable character in *Super Mario Bros. 2*, *Super Smash Bros.*, and her standalone titles has cemented her status as a pop-culture icon and a beloved heroine in her own right.
