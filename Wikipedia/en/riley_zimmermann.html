Riley Peter Zimmermann (born September 24, 1993) is an American classical and contemporary double bassist and music educator. Known for his work in orchestral settings and chamber music, Zimmermann served as the Acting Principal Bass of the Richmond Symphony from 2023 to 2025. He is based in the Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania area.

Education and Early Career

A native of Dillsburg, Pennsylvania, Zimmermann completed his collegiate schooling in Pittsburgh. He holds a Bachelor's degree in Music Education from Duquesne University and a Master’s degree in Double Bass Performance from Carnegie Mellon University. His principal teachers included members of the Pittsburgh Symphony bass section.

As a student and young professional, he freelanced extensively, performing with orchestras such as the Cincinnati Symphony, Sarasota Opera, and the New World Symphony. In 2019, he won 1st place in the Orchestral Division competition at the International Society of Bassists convention.

Orchestral and Chamber Work

Zimmermann’s primary focus has been on orchestral performance. His most notable appointment was as the Acting Principal Bass of the Richmond Symphony. Beyond the classical realm, he is active in other musical styles, including bluegrass and Celtic music, having toured with bands such as The Town Pants and the Jakob’s Ferry Stragglers.

Contraduo

He co-founded the double bass duo Contraduo with his wife, fellow bassist Emily Tarantino. The duo performs original arrangements and compositions throughout Western Pennsylvania and has also conducted educational workshops.

Music Education

In addition to performing, Zimmermann maintains a busy teaching schedule. He has taught elementary orchestra in the North Allegheny School District and served as interim bass faculty at Seton Hill University. Since 2019, he has been on the faculty of Bass Works, a specialized double bass camp based in Baltimore, Maryland.
