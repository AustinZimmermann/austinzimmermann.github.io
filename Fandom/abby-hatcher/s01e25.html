Plot Summary

Part 1: Fuzzlies’ New Year Dragon

Abby is excited for the Chinese New Year celebration and is determined to make it the absolute best for her beloved Wai Po (maternal grandmother). To celebrate the holiday, Abby leads the Fuzzlies in an elaborate and festive dragon dance performance. As always, Fuzzly mishaps and their unique abilities complicate the practice and the performance. Abby, with the help of Bozzly and the rest of the Fuzzlies, must use a "Brain Spark" to keep the traditional celebration running smoothly and honor her Wai Po’s visit. This segment emphasizes family, cultural traditions, and teamwork.

Part 2: Full Moon Fuzzly

Teeny Terry, the small, inflating Fuzzly, becomes fascinated when he learns about the "Fuzzly in the Moon" during a super full and bright moon. Convinced he needs a closer look, Teeny Terry attempts to reach the moon. Abby realizes Teeny Terry’s intense curiosity could lead to trouble and, along with Bozzly, works to find a way to let Teeny Terry see the moon’s surface clearly and satisfy his imagination without actually leaving the planet. The magical effects of the full moon often cause the Fuzzlies to become extra fuzzy, playful, and sometimes mischievous, adding to the challenge of the night.

Featured Fuzzlies

  • Bozzly: Abby’s best Fuzzly friend and partner, essential in both the dragon dance and the moon mission.
  • Teeny Terry: The primary Fuzzly in the second segment, whose balloon-like inflation power is key to his attempts to reach the moon.
  • Princess Flug: Likely contributes glitter goo (sticky or slippery) to the New Year Dragon dance chaos.
  • Otis: His extendable tentacles would be helpful (or chaotic) in the dragon dance construction.
  • Mo and Bo: Their stretchy torsos and rhyming commentary often accompany group activities.
